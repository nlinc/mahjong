How to Play American Mahjong

The Absolute Basics

American Mahjong is played with 4 players and 152 tiles. The goal is to be the first to build a hand of 14 tiles that matches one of the exact hands on the selected annual card. This app currently includes a compact practice card for learning and play-testing.

Tile Composition

  • Suits: Dots 🔴, Bams 🎋, Crakes 🀇 (numbers 1-9, four of each)
  • Winds: East, South, West, North (four of each)
  • Dragons: Green, Red, White (Sope) (four of each)
  • Flowers: 8 special flower tiles (always counts as value cards, cannot be used in numerical runs)
  • Jokers: 8 wild tiles. Can replace any tile in a Pung (3), Kong (4), Quint (5), or Sextet (6). Cannot be used for singles or pairs!

Step 1: The Charleston (Passing Tiles)

At the start, you pass unwanted tiles to build your hand strategy. The mandatory phase passes tiles to: Right, then Across, then Left. If all players agree, a second optional set is performed: Left, Across, Right. Finally, you can perform an optional Courtesy Pass with the player across.

Step 2: Drawing & Discarding

Players take turns drawing a tile from the wall and discarding one. If a tile is discarded, anyone can claim it if it completes their meld:

  • Pung: 3 identical tiles
  • Kong: 4 identical tiles
  • Quint: 5 identical tiles (requires Jokers)
  • Sextet: 6 identical tiles (requires Jokers)

To claim a discard, click the call button. You must immediately expose the completed meld on your rack. You cannot claim sequences (Chows).

Step 3: Declaring Mahjong

When your hand has 14 tiles matching a pattern on the card, call MAHJONG! If you match the hand, you win the round.
